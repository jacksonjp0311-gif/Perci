# Sacred geometry curriculum

This curriculum is deliberately layered. Perci should learn the mathematical
structure of a figure, the historical or ritual context in which a tradition
uses it, and the boundary between evidence and interpretation. It must not
turn a symbolic association into a universal physical claim.

## Core layers

1. **Mathematical structure** — circles, squares, polygons, symmetry,
   transformations, tessellations, proportions, regular polyhedra, and exact
   constructions.
2. **Cultural practice** — mandalas, yantras, temple plans, Islamic geometric
   ornament, calligraphy, vegetal pattern, orientation, and ritual use.
3. **Interpretive layer** — center/periphery, order, unity, cosmos, ascent,
   containment, repetition, and transformation, always attributed to a
   tradition and source.
4. **Epistemic boundary** — a pattern can be mathematically real and
   symbolically meaningful without proving that it encodes a universal energy,
   healing power, or hidden causal law.

## Weight-resident concepts

- `sacred_geometry`: cross-cultural category, not a single doctrine.
- `mandala`: cosmic or deity-abode diagram used in visualization in Buddhist
  contexts; center/periphery and square/circle are symbolic structures.
- `yantra`: ritual diagram whose geometry is interpreted through Indic textual
  and ritual context.
- `platonic_solids`: exactly five convex regular solids; cultural element
  correspondences are later interpretations.
- `golden_ratio`: phi and its mathematical relations to pentagons, pentagrams,
  decagons, and dodecahedra; presence is not proof of intentional design.
- `tessellation`: gapless repetition of shapes; construction and symbolism are
  separate claims.
- `construction`: reproducible generation from circles, squares, triangles,
  stars, or polygons.
- `center_periphery`: a portable visual relation whose cosmological meaning is
  tradition-dependent.
- `symbolic_layers`: mathematical, aesthetic, architectural, and ritual layers
  must not be collapsed into one explanation.
- `sacred_space`: alignment and proportion can integrate symbolic and practical
  planning; historical evidence determines the interpretation.

## Source anchors

- [The Metropolitan Museum of Art: Geometric Patterns in Islamic Art](https://www.metmuseum.org/fr/essays/geometric-patterns-in-islamic-art)
  describes circles, squares, stars, and polygons as repeat units and explains
  how construction, repetition, and interlacing formed complex ornament.
- [Smithsonian: Platonic Solids](https://www.si.edu/spotlight/geometric-models/platonic-solids)
  and [MathWorld: Platonic Solid](https://mathworld.wolfram.com/PlatonicSolid.html)
  support the exact count of five convex regular solids.
- [MathWorld: Golden Ratio](https://mathworld.wolfram.com/GoldenRatio.html)
  supports the mathematical definition and its occurrence in pentagon,
  pentagram, decagon, and dodecahedron relations.
- [Smarthistory: Four Mandalas of the Vajravali Cycle](https://smarthistory.org/four-mandalas-vajravali-cycle/)
  describes Buddhist mandalas as geometric representations of a cosmos and
  aids to visualization.
- [The Metropolitan Museum of Art: Mandalas: Mapping the Buddhist Art of Tibet](https://www.metmuseum.org/es/met-publications/mandalas-mapping-the-buddhist-art-of-tibet)
  frames the mandala as a diagram or map used in Buddhist religious practice.
- [UNESCO: Koh Ker nomination dossier](https://whc.unesco.org/document/186494)
  documents a case where geometry, topography, astronomy, geography, hydrology,
  and religious symbolism were integrated in a sacred urban plan.

## Promotion rule

These concepts are first built as a candidate weight artifact. Promotion
requires held-out prompts with unseen terminology, near-neighbor distractors,
historical-context questions, mathematical construction questions, and
abstention tests for unsupported metaphysical claims. A larger artifact is not
accepted as smarter unless it improves route accuracy, concept margin, transfer,
and explanation while preserving the existing dialogue and exact-tool gates.
